Feedback

Below is some feedback on my presentation above, by my course tutor:

  • clear slides, fast talking, good communication with the audience.
  • nicely explained influences and reflection on the problem.
  • design evaluation criteria defined
  • excellent concept
  • 3D print prototype demonstrates engineering skills
  • too many slides for 5 mins - some editing skills needed

What I learned from the presentation is that it is often better to say less and get through everything in an engaging, in-depth way. It is true that I have a fast, loud and rush-like way of giving presentations and explaining stuff in general, and although it can sometimes be good to be the 'loud, enthusiastic' speaker, it is a 'must-do' to let the viewers - and myself - breathe from time to time. Targets for the future:

  • remember: less is more!
  • learn how to be selective and don't worry about cutting some stuff
  • breathe
  • identify the most important things and make sure to have enough time to talk about them (in this case, these could be the design evaluation criteria and 3D printed model)
  • 3D print prototype demonstrates engineering skills
  • too many slides for 5 mins - some editing skills needed
