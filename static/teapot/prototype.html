Below are a set of notes that I took during the early stages of the prototype-making. [Notetaking on iPad in Madrid, December 2013]:

This was my guide through firing temperatures. My teapot was fired at 1150°.

Making my teapot out of Terracota Clay

I've made the prototype with terracotta clay due to its favourable thermal properties that facilitate heat retention. I used two hemispherical moulds and a pottery wheel to make the containers and spouts respectively. Instead of cutting off the piece of wall between the hemisphere and the spout, I 'pinched' small holes through it in order to enable the filtering of loose tea leaves. Once the teapot was made, it was then fired at 1150°C. The next stage was the adventure of flying with it back to London, where it was then glazed with grey satin glaze and fired for the last time at a lower temperature of 850°C.
